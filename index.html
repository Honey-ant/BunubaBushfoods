Bunuba Muwayi (country) extends over a vast area of the centeral Kimberly, in the north-west of Australia. Bunuba people have been here, looking after this country for many thousands of years, and continue doing so today. They do this by spending time on country, keeping culture and landuage alive through each generation and using cultural land management methods light right-way winthali(fire). Bunuba people developed a plan for protecing country --- it is called Jalangurru Muwayi: Bunuba Healthy Country Plan. The Plan has informeation about what is important to Bunuba people, including a seasonal calendar, threats and targets (things that need to be protected to keep people and country happy).


The Bunuba Seasons

  • Flowering and fruiting times for each plant are indicated in the calendar showing the months of the year and the Bunuba seasons
  • Bulurru is wet season
  • Girinybali is when the rains finish
  • Nganali is cold weather
  • Barranggais hot time and the build up to Bulurru
  • The letters on the inner circle indicate the months of the year. For example May, June and July are all in Nganali
